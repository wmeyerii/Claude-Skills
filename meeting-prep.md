# Meeting Prep Brief

Prep for a meeting by researching attendees, surfacing open items, and generating talking points.

## When to Use
- Before any investor, partner, broker, or vendor call
- When you need background on someone you haven't met
- When you need to surface open items before a follow-up
- When you have context (emails, notes, docs) to synthesize fast

## Required Inputs
1. **Who** — name and company of primary attendee
2. **Topic** — what the meeting is about
3. **Context** — any materials you have: email thread, Salesforce note, Notion page, LinkedIn profile, prior meeting notes

If no context is provided, offer to web search the person and company.

## Workflow

**Step 1 — Parse what you have**
Extract: name, role, company, background, prior interactions, open items, stated interests.

**Step 2 — Fill gaps with web search** (if connected)
Search name + company for recent news, LinkedIn, notable deals. Note source and date.

**Step 3 — Build the brief**

## Output Format

```
MEETING PREP BRIEF
──────────────────
Meeting: [name] · [company] · [topic] · [date/time]

BACKGROUND
[2–4 sentences on who they are]

HISTORY
· [most recent interaction]
· [prior interaction]

OPEN ITEMS
· [unresolved item 1]
· [unresolved item 2]

TALKING POINTS
1. [specific, grounded talking point]
2. [specific, grounded talking point]
3. [specific, grounded talking point]

FOLLOW-UP TEMPLATE
Subject: [suggested subject line]
[2–3 sentence follow-up draft]
```

## Quality Gate
- Background is specific to this person, not generic
- Talking points tied to known history or stated interests
- Open items sourced from real materials, not invented
- Follow-up template ready to send with minimal editing
- Output fits on one screen — no filler
